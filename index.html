Ottawa Accessibility Unconference

Saturday, December 6th 2014 from 8:30 AM until 5:00 PM
At Carleton University

A day of informal discussions, demos and debates concerning access to artifacts in our digital age.
Admission is free & everyone is invited.

Blind Trust in Cryptographic Tools



Abstract:

A demonstration and comparison of file and mail encryption tools when they operated without eyesight. After the demonstration we hope to spark a discussion about blind trust in our tools.

Where do we go to access to an understanding of encryption tools? Can we have trust without an understanding?


Audience:

This session is suitable for everyone.

End of user



Abstract:

People are stripped of their dignity, imagination and their place in the world when technologists mark them as “users”. Radical applications of assistive technologies, uncontrollable programs, obsolete computer programmers and your grand-papa are challenging the common delusion that “users” are too stupid to take care of themselves.

The session will consist of an examination of user-centric artifacts and an informal discussion. Participants are encouraged to bring their own artifacts for show and tell.

We might open the discussion with two questions: Who is operating the systems we make and maintain? And when will the anglo-techno-culture stop insulting each-other with derogatory user-centric language?


Audience:

This session is suitable for everyone.
